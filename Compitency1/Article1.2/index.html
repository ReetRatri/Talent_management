  • Try exploring and understanding others' concerns by putting yourself in others' shoes. This is key to better understanding the customers' motives but also to find an appropriate solution to their situations or challenges.

Here are some further development tips to consider:

If a person rushes to respond or share their opinions, they stand in the way of empathy.

Instead, hold mock conversations in which one person talks while the other hears them out completely.

Remember to:

  • Encourage the listener to face the speaker and make eye contact.
  • Try your best to understand what the other person is saying, do not simply listen to reply.
  • Listen with an open mind and without prejudice. Be respectful of others.

Those learning empathy need feedback to determine if they understand others' messages during communication.

Saying what the person said back to them in a different way helps them double-check that they got the right message.

  • Paraphrasing may sound like, “From what I'm hearing, you seem shocked and upset about your doctor’s exam results. Is that right?”
  • If the message was not received correctly, the speaker can try to relay it again so that the listener understands.

Identifying what someone is feeling can be challenging.

Reflect and analyze other details, like facial expressions, body language, tone of voice, and the actual words.

Pay attention and show interest in what others are telling you because it is not enough to know what the other person feels, but we have to show them you care.

Is someone around you in need of empathy?

If so, use open body language that fosters a connection, make occasional eye contact, and soften your voice.

Consider sharing your thoughts and feelings when the situation is appropriate to allows exercise of empathy.

When you are talking to others, make an effort to use feeling words. This helps clue them into your emotional state.

Self-awareness is the ability to see yourself clearly and objectively through reflection and introspection. It can make us more proactive, boost our acceptance, and encourage positive self-development.

To increase self–awareness try the following:

  • Mindfulness refers to being present in the moment and paying attention to yourself and your surroundings rather than getting lost in thought or ruminating or daydreaming.
  • Meditation is the practice of focusing your attention on one thing, such as your breath or a feeling, and letting your thoughts drift by instead of holding on to them.
  • Both practices can help you become more aware of your internal state and your reactions to things. They can also help you identify your thoughts and feelings and keep from getting so caught up in them that you lose your hold on your “self.

Be willing to accept differences with others, be tolerant, patient and kind with those around you and with yourself.
