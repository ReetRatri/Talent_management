Closing the Skills Gap

Welcome!​

Now that you have completed the skills gap exercise and have had an initial discussion with your manager, you can explore the competencies for your role that need to develop in order to succeed further in your current position and the organization.

This e-module has been designed to provide you with bite-sized development tips to reflect on. This is the start of your development journey and closing the skills gap.

X

Let's get started!

Take the following 5 steps to unlock the development content suitable for you.

The following advice should be approached with cultural and personal views in mind. For some, giving direct eye contact may be more natural than others. The advice below should be taken as a recommendation (subject to cultural and social acceptance/context). For any market third-party workers (TPWs), this module is recommended as best practice only.

Step 1

Choose the organization and role you are currently in

Step 2

Click from the relevant areas and select the competencies you wish to understand, explore further and develop.

Step 3

Focus on up to 3 competencies to begin developing and practising the tips in your current role

Step 4

For each competency you explore, you will - learn the definition, understand the positive behaviours associated with the competency that defines success, recap the development tips that are from your individual skills gap report, uncover detailed development tips for exploring and considering ways in which they can be practised in your current role/personal life

Step 5

Once you have explored the competencies needed, you can download the developmental self-reflection plan that gives you a chance to pause and think deeply about your past achievements and future growth areas.​

Creating a Development Plan for Success

Once you have explored the competencies needed, you can download the developmental self-reflection plan that allows you to pause and think deeply about your past achievements and future growth areas.

This plan will allow you to reflect on how you would like to develop and set goals and managers' guidance on how they can support you in achieving them.

It is recommended that you use the developmental self-reflection plan to inform the development plan template available in your individual skills gap report, which can also be downloaded here.

Please note: for third-party workers, this is best practice only.
