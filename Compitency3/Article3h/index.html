Managing Activities

Efficiently organizing own or team activities,
allocating appropriate resources and keeping
a global vision of all duties and activities

Positive Behaviours
  • Efficiently manages and splits time between all duties.
  • Successfully delegates tasks in order to keep things done fluently.
  • Keeps track of what others are doing and stays informed about
    their needs.
  • Keeps the team and supervisor updated about figures and
    actions delivered.
  • Has a high view of tasks to perform on mid term.

Two-Minute Rule

Apply the “two-minute” rule – implementing this simple rule in your life is the first step towards better self-administration.

It teaches you to avoid procrastination habits. Be efficient.

If a task requires less than 2 minutes to complete, do not delay it; instead, do it now and keep crossing things from your to-do list.

Focusing on Your Strengths

Unleash your potential – discover what you are best at.

Focusing on your strengths will help you manage things well and bring happiness and mental peace.

Myth of Multitasking

Forget the myth of multitasking – do one thing at a time by putting your heart and soul into it to enjoy success.

Manage your time and efforts and move ahead once done with the task to have a hassle-free life at work and home.

Work Ethics

Develop good work ethics – live by your value.

  • Organize your work.
  • Set up a reminder app on your phone or create a to-do list.
  • Avoid distractions to fulfill your responsibilities.

Speak and Listen

Speak up – people cannot read your mind.

Speak and share your thoughts and ideas to bring harmony, coordinate, and establish friendly relationships.

Speak and listen!

Timeliness

Be self-organized and follow deadlines – maintain your schedule.

Be responsible and complete all assigned tasks within the set deadline.

Strictly follow checkpoints to fulfill your commitments. Timeliness is the most prized value.

Be Considerate

Be considerate –to gain respect, first, you should consider others.

Practise courtesy and politeness.

A well-mannered person is a most-loved person. Follow the golden rule of respecting others.

Admitting Mistakes

Admit your mistakes – to err is human.

There is no harm in admitting mistakes.

Admitting them instead of avoiding them is the healthiest way to grow, so don’t be afraid to own your mistakes.

Look After Your Hygiene

Stay healthy and ensure your well-being – look after your hygiene.

Health is wealth.

  • Maintain it by adopting a healthy lifestyle.
  • Eat an organic and balanced diet.
  • Make exercise a regular part of your routine.
  • Take small breaks and take part in any physical activity.

Self-Reflection

Take time for self-reflection – evaluate yourself.

It can be how to amend your mistake, acknowledge your achievements, set yourself up for accepting new challenges, and unlock more doors to success.
