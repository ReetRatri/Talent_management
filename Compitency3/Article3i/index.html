Relationship Building

Going further than the minimum interactions and engaging on a deeper level with others. Building a climate of trust and maintaining a friendly and casual atmosphere

Positive Behaviours
  • Personalizes each interaction with customers.
  • Is welcoming, offering a qualitative experience to customers.
  • Tries to find common ground with customers.
  • Is transparent with customers about the products and what they can or cannot do.
  • Remembers regular customers’ business details and scope.

Development Tips for Improving – RELATIONSHIP BUILDING

As per the individual skills gap report, try the following:

  • Shadowing a colleague that is expert in building relationship with customers, focusing on the most successful behaviors, then trying to reproduce them starting with the ones that come more naturally.
  • Try to remember preferences or information you learnt about a customer, and try to leverage them next time you meet customer.

One of the essential soft skills for building successful work relationships is communication. Developing practical communication skills can positively influence how you interact with others.

For example, you might practice active listening and interpreting non-verbal communication like body language. You also develop your communication skills by seeking opportunities to join conversations or contribute to team meetings.

Another key factor in successful business relationships is empathy. When you show empathy to your peers you are showing them you care, that you understand their feelings and point of view and that you have respect for other people's ideas and emotions. Practicing empathy can help you positively impact how you get along with your teammates and other colleagues.

Developing emotional intelligence is another effective way to ensure you are building positive work relationships. You can develop your emotional intelligence by practicing empathy, learning to understand your teammates' feelings and paying close attention to the dynamics in your workplace. Doing this can help increase your emotional awareness, both for your own feelings as well as those of your colleagues.

Asking for feedback can be highly beneficial to your professional development. Communicating your desire to use advice for professional improvement shows that you are motivated to succeed in your job. It also shows that you are open to new ideas, learning new skills or are willing to improve. This can positively impact your relationship skills.

  • Another way you can further develop your relationship-building skills is to get involved with different activities in your workplace. For instance, you might join a group of colleagues for coffee during lunch or join an informal meeting to provide insight to your other teammates. You can improve your relationship-building technique by getting involved with your coworkers and contributing to various activities going on in the office.
  • You might also consider the following tips to help you apply effective relationship-building skills at work.
    • Contribute to your team as often as you can, including in new projects, team-building exercises and sharing ideas with your coworkers.
    • Deal with conflict openly and professionally with clear communication and empathy so you can solve problems quickly and efficiently.
    • Avoid gossip in the workplace by only entertaining professional and work-related conversations.
    • Practice positive emotional reactions and seek out mentoring opportunities during times you receive constructive criticism from your supervisors.
    • Find ways to assist or help your team and coworkers when you see it is needed.
