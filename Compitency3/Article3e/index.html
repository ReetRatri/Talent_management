Conscientiousness

Paying attention to details and to work environment; being aware of signals and respectful of rules. Being detail focused, careful, diligent and dutiful and applying those high standards to self and others

Positive Behaviours
  • Follows rules and guidelines.
  • Acts accordingly with specific country regulation (if applies).
  • Keeps the same care and diligence repeating same or similar steps and process several times a day.
  • Makes sure their environment is clean and spotless.
  • Is detail-oriented when observing their environment or the client.

Development Tips for Improving – CONSCIENTIOUSNESS

As per the individual skills gap report, try the following:

  • Focusing more on details and reviewing documents explaining rules and legislation essential to your job.
  • Work on a quality checklist to ensure that you respect all steps in line with high-quality organizational standards.

Here are seven steps you can take to be more conscientious:

You can be reliable at work by completing tasks with a consistent level of quality, actively communicating with team members, and practising self-management.

In addition, conscientious employees are aware that the quality of their work reflects the organization and that other people depend on them to do their job.

For example, suppose you encounter an issue that may prevent you from submitting work on time for a group project.

In that case, you can communicate proactively with team members and let them know about the problem immediately to allow them time to adjust accordingly.

You can improve your work ethic by focusing entirely on each current task, managing your time so that you can complete tasks, and creating a plan for more difficult or complex projects.

In addition, eliminating distractions can help you make meaningful progress in your work and focus more fully so that you perform a thorough job.

For example, if you work on a computer, consider adding an extension to your browser to block yourself from accessing distracting websites during the day.

You can prioritize organization at work by upcoming recording deadlines on your calendar. You may also approach each workday with a to-do list of essential tasks and daily goals and plan to work on specific tasks at scheduled times.

You may also schedule time as necessary to organize your emails, papers, and workspace to make files and messages easy to find.

If you're aware of what you need to prioritize each day and gauge how much time your responsibilities may take, you can better avoid distractions and make meaningful progress in your work.

You can practice punctuality by being on time or early for work and meetings.

Ensure that you have reliable transportation to work, and create reminders 10 minutes before an appointment so that you have time to get to it or log on if it's a virtual meeting.

You can also practice punctuality by setting reminders on your work calendar for upcoming deadlines.

This initiative can help you allocate the time you need to work on projects.

In addition, practicing punctuality indicates professionalism and shows that you value other people's time.

  • For example, consider allowing extra time for your commute to ensure you stay punctual even if you encounter a delay. For example, if you have several meetings in a row, you can leave additional time between sessions to be on time for your next appointment, even if one finishes later than you expect.

It's easier to shift your behaviour if you think doing something is essential and enjoyable and aligns with your values.

For example, if answering emails seems like a trivial part of your job compared to tasks that require more time and deep thought, think of how answering emails is an important task and how it could align with your values.

If responding to general inquiries promptly reflects positively on the company and may bring in potential new clients, you can view it as an essential task that can provide satisfaction.

One way to set realistic goals is to imagine the steps toward achieving the goal, plan out the necessary steps, and identify any obstacles.

When you set realistic goals, you’re practising conscientiousness by being mindful of where you spend your time.

If the path to achieving the goal seems challenging, you may re-evaluate how you can get to your final step before you start working toward it.

For example, you may initially invest time gathering necessary materials and planning the individual steps before executing your plan.

You can build a stronger connection with your colleagues by interacting with them regularly and positively.

Practise mindful communication by active listening, paying attention to your tone, and communicating your thoughts clearly and respectfully.

Ask your team members questions about how they're doing, what they're working on, and what they enjoy doing outside the workplace.
