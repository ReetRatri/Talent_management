Development Tips for Improving – BUSINESS ACUMEN/FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT

As per the individual skills gap report, try the following:

  • Use a logical and systematic approach to financial data collection and analysis. You could include finances as an integrated part of your business development strategy: analyzing a problem or issue from multiple angles, using multiple sources of data will allow you to have a better understanding of the issue.
  • Seek advice from other team members on successful methods that have been adopted to forecast and build related plans.
  • Conduct the analysis of your territory considering all financial elements and discuss your insights with your manager or organize a session with key staff to present financial data in a clear and concise format.
  • Create forums for staff to meet and share financial analysis on the performance of a specific customer.

Get to Know Your Company’s Business Strategies

Understanding your organization's business model is essential for developing strong business acumen.

Several components go into a business model, including:

  • How the supply chain operates?
  • What is the supply chain's business cycle?
  • How the company makes money?
  • How does the company carry out talent acquisition?

The better you understand these business factors, the more you develop your business acumen and use it to improve your business decision.

Understand the Financial Side of Business

You don't need a background in finance to learn your way around the financial side of an organization.

Ways to acquire knowledge on how finances affect a company include:

  • Tracking key metrics
  • Analyzing how the key metrics impact the organization
  • Gaining knowledge from well-known business financial resources available online

Invest in Education

Education is a great way to enhance your business acumen.

There are several online courses and certifications you can pursue to broaden your business acumen skills and become more effective in the workplace.

Find a Mentor

​Another way to improve your business acumen and knowledge is to find and work with a mentor already established in your industry. ​

Even if you cannot meet with them, you can still read their blog posts or follow them on social media to learn more about how they operate and the knowledge they possess.

Here are a few ways to use your business acumen skills in the workplace:

A great way to implement your business acumen skills at work and better understand your company's customers is to perform qualitative and quantitative research.

Collecting customer insight gives you a better understanding of market orientation and allows you to use your business acumen skills to meet these customers' needs successfully and profitably.

You are collecting data from customers, sending out customer surveys, speaking directly with customers, and all great ways to perform customer research and gain valuable knowledge.

Another critical component of expressing business acumen is listening to your company's customers and implementing strategies to improve upon challenging areas.

For example, the more you know about your customers, the better you'll be able to identify a successful way for your company to deliver products and services that attract clients.

Listening to customers also improves other business acumen skills, including communication and marketing skills.

A great way to utilize your business acumen skills in the workplace is to stay up-to-date on the latest industry news and use this knowledge when making business decisions.

Keep track of business news and research how other companies in your industry respond to changes.

Make a Budget—and Stick to it

Do you know where all your money goes? Do you know how much money you spend on things like going out to eat, seeing a movie, buying beer, or purchasing clothes?

Most people don’t.

Are you one of those who pray daily that you don’t overdraw your bank account?

If so, make your budget.

Go back through your bank statements for the last year and write down how much you spent in each category.

Identify any problem areas, patterns over days, months and solve how best to manage the budget, especially for the times of uncertainty costs that may arise.

Be a Conscious Consumer

When you go to the grocery store, do you have a list? Do you look at prices? Do you use coupons? Many online resources and apps can help you focus more on your spending.

Be aware of every single cent you spend!

When people don’t do that, their money tends to evaporate.

It takes a bit of effort to look for coupons, make lists, and examine the prices at the stores where you shop, but it’s worth it in the long run.

And it makes a BIG difference.

Mathematical Proficiency

An important aspect of financial management is being able to work with large sums of money.

Furthermore, you should be able to analyze data and understand formulas.

Sometimes, knowledge of international finance may even help you understand diverse financial documents.

You can try to read the financial section of the newspaper and use it as a case study to be exposed to various financial information, as well to analyze the data, paying attention to the details in your spare time to help you practice this mathematical component.

Technological Skills

Many financial managers today make use of various technological applications to ease their work.

It has gone beyond using just ledgers and spreadsheets. In today’s digital world, you need technical skills to manage your finances.

  • Take some time to research the various technological applications available that could help both in your personal life and professional career.
  • Try conducting a comparison exercise on the different applications so you are fully equipped with what is available or coming in today’s digital world.
