Sales

Since business development directly impacts a sales teamâ€™s success, having basic sales skills can help you succeed in a business development role.

Business development professionals target potential growth areas and persuade others to follow their lead.

They must follow up on opportunities and sell ideas and strategies rather than specific items. They also need interpersonal skills to build relationships with potential partners or prospects

To achieve their growth objectives, business development professionals must be comfortable cold-calling or setting up in-person meetings to discuss potential opportunities and sell the idea of a partnership that benefits all involved parties.

Additional sales skills include:

  • Prospecting
  • Keeping the sales funnel updated
  • Making sure the CRM or customer management platform is up to date
  • Qualifying potential leads
  • Ensuring relationships with clients are warm, respectful, and continually nurtured

Communication

Strong communication skills mean a more confident and effective business development professional.

If you can confidently present your goals and justifications and produce clearly written documents and emails, this will help others understand your goals better.

Similarly, you will build trust and form a solid working bond by listening attentively to your clients, colleagues, or customers.

Some specific communication skills you should develop as a business development professional include:

  • Presentation and pitching skills
  • Active listening
  • Understanding body language
  • Business writing
  • Interpersonal skills like empathy and respect
  • Visual communication technology skills

Negotiation

Though a business developer may not finalize any deals, they should be able to negotiate with potential clients and partners to understand if their organization can get a good deal early on in the vetting process.

To succeed in negotiations, you need to think creatively, understand the needs of others, and prioritize the goals of the potential contract.

Marketing

A goal of a business developer is to promote a company and engage with others who might be interested in partnerships.

Therefore, you should understand and apply the basic marketing principles in your business development role.

In this role, you may find ways to promote the brand, expand the market, acquire new users and generate awareness.

Depending on the size of your organization, you may or may not have the assistance of an entire marketing team.

Additionally, many business development executives seek opportunities to form partnerships rather than sell to a direct end customer. Therefore, the ability to promote the brand effectively is crucial.

Some specific marketing skills you should develop include:

  • Email marketing
  • Social media management
  • Writing cold emails and other outreach correspondence
  • Utilising target audience and consumer personas to identify and communicate with leads effectively

Business Intelligence

Business intelligence is about gaining a thorough understanding of your business's market.

This can be from gaining professional experience or collecting and studying data you or another team have gathered.

Some of the most common data types that benefit a business developer include the size of the potential market and what might affect it.

In addition, it is essential to know the marketing campaigns that can produce the desired responses from specific markets.

You may start by researching, collecting data, and learning more about target markets to improve your business intelligence skills.

Some business intelligence skills include:

  • Collecting, organizing, and reviewing data
  • Making data-driven decisions
  • Using data visualization

Analysing ROI Data

Successful businesses need to measure returns on investments (ROI) and be able to report on successes and areas for improvement when presenting to managers and executives.

This means clearly stating what you have done, how much it costs in quantities such as time and money, and what benefits your actions have had to the company.

The metrics business developers use include revenue generated, the number of deals produced through partnerships, and their influence on the sales pipeline.

Therefore, it is essential to be able to track company progress and regularly update on any business development plans that you are working towards.

  • Using sales tracking software
  • Making accurate insights based on data
  • Presenting data clearly

Project Management

Developing a business is a large and complex project, and you need to be able to manage many aspects to be a successful business developer.

You can use many project management skills to handle leads effectively, monitor prospects, time correspondence, and set development goals.

Standard project management skills needed to be a business development professional include:

  • Leadership
  • Team management
  • Strategic thinking
  • Risk management
  • Delegation and resource allocation
  • Time management
