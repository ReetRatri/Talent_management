Digital Literacy

Interacting with and leveraging technology and digital tools. Using systems (including hardware and software) to manage daily tasks. Strong understanding of existing and emerging Digital technologies.

Positive Behaviours
  • Has a basic knowledge of Salesforce
  • Has a basic knowledge of Excel
  • Uses a tablet or PC in their basic functions
  • Organizes virtual calls and webinars with customers and final users
    if needed
  • Troubleshoots and solves basic problems on apps
  • Supports customers in solving basic problems on apps

Development Tips for Improving - DIGITAL LITERACY

As per the individual skills gap report, try the following:

  • Attend an internal and external training session on the use of digital tools that general and specific.
  • Shadow a colleague who is highly skilled in the use of digital tools when interacting with customers.
  • Conduct some analysis on some mock data and share the main learnings afterwards with a colleague or manager who can provide feedback.
  • Stretch yourself in using new functionalities in Excel to run your analysis.
  • Take the opportunity to teach a new hire on the digital tools that the workforce is using on daily basis.

Use Technology in Your Everyday Life

Start using technology in your everyday life, even for simple tasks such as sending emails or researching information online. The more you use technology, the more comfortable and confident you will become with it.

Here are some workplace examples:

  • Use digital tools to create digital content this may include creating a new database to manage complex data enabling easy retrieval for self and others.
  • Analyse, interpret and critically evaluate the credibility of online content when drawing on data or information relating to their area of work.
  • Enhances productivity through the use of digital tools, e.g. using online calendars, digital prioritisation, or mind mapping tools.
  • Experiments with and evaluates a range of digital tools and features to improve the quality and sophistication of their work.
  • Shares relevant online and digital content with colleagues using appropriate channels (e.g. by uploading content to a digital workspace, or by inserting hyperlinks in shared documents).
  • Uses work management tools or project planning software to collaborate with colleagues.
  • Communicates with colleagues, customers and suppliers in a range of online and digital platforms using clear, concise and respectful language appropriate to the context.

Take an Online Course

There are many online courses available that can teach you the basics of using different types of digital devices and software.

Then, once you’ve completed a course, you can move on to more advanced topics.

Read Blogs and Articles

Read blogs and articles about emerging technology and how to use it effectively.

This will help you stay up-to-date with the latest trends and learn new tips and tricks.

Join an Online Community

There are many online communities dedicated to technology and digital literacy.

Joining one of these communities will give you access to helpful resources and allow you to connect with others who are also interested in developing their digital literacy skills.

Use Social Media

Social media is a great way to practice your digital literacy skills.

Use it to connect with friends and family, share information and content, and stay up-to-date with the latest news and trends.
