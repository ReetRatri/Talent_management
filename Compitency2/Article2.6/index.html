Account Management

Managing relationships with key customer accounts of an area or territory to develop account plans that clearly define customer opportunities, strategies and actions, to achieve consumer, channel and financial objectives whilst delivering mutual, profitable, value growth

Positive Behaviours
  • Assesses account/s potential.
  • Sets priorities in account development based on potential.​
  • Builds specific strategy in new products and services for
    account or territory managed.
  • Systematically monitors revenue objectives.

Development Tips for Improving – ACCOUNT MANAGEMENT

As per the individual skills gap report, try the following:

  • Work in teams to develop this competency. You can discuss account management operation policies with your supervisors, or even prepare sales performance reports by collecting and analyzing key customer information and share lessons learned with colleagues. They also might help you to understand the working relationships with key accounts.
  • Conduct post-meeting debriefs with a peer/supervisor/coach could also help you to identify what went well and what improvements are needed.
  • Talk to the key contacts of your accounts to better understand the drivers for their satisfaction.
  • Read market analyses for the accounts you are working on and identify sales opportunities.
  • Coach a team member on strategies to maximize return on investment from account management activities.

Tips for Applying Your Account Management Skills in a Work Environment

The primary focus for this type of manager is to provide excellent service and a positive experience for their clients.

The following are some suggestions on improving how you apply your skills in a workspace:

Take a Proactive Approach When Assisting a Client With Their Needs

One way to stay proactive is by meeting with professionals and colleagues from other departments who have similar clients.

Then, try to figure out what these clients usually request or need.

This can help you develop strategies and action plans to meet your clients' future necessities.

Keep Yourself Reliably Available for Your Employees and Clients to Contact

If the client requests or has concerns about a project or service, they trust you to communicate this clearly to the employees.

Similarly, if employees find it challenging to complete a task for a client, they may trust you to relay this to a client.

They’ll likely expect a logical solution to the problem as well.

Focus on Building Stronger Interpersonal Relationships With Your Clients

Prioritise forming and maintaining more profound relationships with your clients by consistently checking in to ensure they're being taken care of.

You might also inform them that you're readily available if they need you to perform extra tasks. It's also important to tell them about regular updates on new developments and improvements.

This makes it easier for the client to create plans of their own.

Provide a Comprehensive Action Plan

Customers better understand how your organization can help overcome their industry challenges if they see it in front of them.

Use your problem-solving and organizational skills to list out plans of action for your clients when starting a new project.

Keep them informed on your team's progress with their project and what to expect from the outcome.
